Story by Season

Season 1

Marta de la Reina, director of the family business: De La Reina perfumeries, lives a life marked by family pressure and loneliness due to an absent marriage. In this context, enters Serafina Valero, a young worker and daughter of the De La Reina family's longtime chauffeur, who soon becomes an unexpected emotional support for Marta after obtaining a job as a shop assistant at the store. The two have known each other since childhood, and the friendship that begins when they start working together quickly transforms into a bond of deep love, culminating in their long-awaited first kiss in the factory warehouse, which marks a turning point in their relationship. As the season progresses, Marta and Fina face the prejudices of the era, the constant risk of being discovered, obstacles such as Marta's husband, her own brother Jesús, and the lack of understanding of their respective fathers. Although their relationship remains a secret, they manage to enjoy moments of great love, intimacy, and freedom.

The first season of Sueños de Libertad (Dreams of Freedom) aired on Antena 3, Monday through Friday at 3:45 PM and consisted of a total of 269 episodes. On September 22, 2025, the series premiered on HBO Latin America, including previously unseen scenes.

Season 2

The relationship between “Mafin” (Marta and Fina) faces a new challenge when Marta, married by arrangement to hotel entrepreneur Pelayo Olivares, must balance her secret relationship with Fina and the appearances Pelayo needs to maintain in order to advance politically in his ambition to become governor. Initially, Pelayo seems to be an ally to the protagonists, but as the episodes unfold, his true interests begin to be exposed through his manipulations until he becomes their main threat.

During a conversation in the neighborhood, Marta tells Fina about some land that Doña Clara, Pelayo's mother, is investing in. Taking advantage of the conversation, Fina reveals her desire to buy her own house with the money she inherited from her father. Together they decide to visit the land, which becomes a turning point for Fina, who discovers her passion for photography when she uses her father's old camera to take pictures of Marta. This hidden talent quickly blossoms thanks to the support of Marta and her friends, allowing her to take photos for the neighborhood daycare calendar and set up her own darkroom. This special corner becomes a refuge where Mafin can live out her love and passion, far from the eyes of the world.

However, happiness and romance are soon interrupted by the shadow of danger. Santiago, the man who had harassed Fina, manages to escape from prison and heads to the Mafinca. In this setting, a confrontation takes place that culminates in Santiago's death, generating a wave of consequences that affect the protagonists. Taking advantage of the situation, Pelayo threatens to incriminate even Marta if she does not disappear. Desperate and fearing for her lover's well-being, Fina makes a drastic decision and suddenly flees, leaving Marta in deep despair and facing the pressure of her surroundings alone. What has been until now a story of resistance and true love ends up shrouded in an uncertain future. The couple, who had fought for their love, now see their bond put to the test in the face of an uncertain future.

Finding herself without Fina, Marta de la Reina goes through a period of painful mourning, turning to her diary and, at times, to alcohol. Pelayo witnesses this process but still does not deign to tell her the truth; instead, he takes her to Madrid with his mother-in-law so she can continue to support him as governor. Upon her return, Marta continues her life of deception, as Pelayo's wife, watching as the factory falls into losses due to sabotage carried out by her cousin Gabriel, ultimately ending up in Brossard's hands. Having acquired a majority stake, the French competitor revolutionizes the factory with changes to logos, uniforms, and even work methods. Damián, as the company’s founder, feels his world crumbling.

Shortly thereafter, Brossard sends an auditor named Cloe Dubois, who is immediately captivated by Marta. Marta and Cloe begin working together and getting to know each other better; they admire one another and start sharing details of their personal lives. It is revealed that Cloe is bisexual. She tries to form a closer bond with Marta but is constantly rejected by her. Pelayo, meanwhile, loses his position as governor after threats to reveal his sexuality, and leaves for Mexico with Darío.

Toward the end of the season, a pivotal situation arises involving Marta's diary: Gabriel has it in his possession and will reveal everything unless Damián sells him his shares. The head of the family must decide whether to lose his position in the company entirely or to protect his daughter.

The season two finale finds Mafin and Marta separated, with Marta living a lie but starting something with Cloe, and Fina in Argentina with a new life we know nothing about.

The second season of Sueños de Libertad (Dreams of Freedom) began airing on Antena 3 on March 20, 2025, and ended on January 15, 2026.

Season 3

The relationship between “Mafin” (Marta and Fina) is on hold at the start of this season, as Marta de la Reina continues to live a lie: she doesn't know that Fina left because of Pelayo's threat.

It has been four months since Brossard became the factory's majority shareholder and the villain Gabriel de la Reina took over as director. Marta is in a relationship with Cloe, chatting in the parks, while she ponders how she can avenge her family for the misfortunes caused by her cousin.

A few weeks later, Marta and Cloe's relationship comes to a definitive end because Marta realizes that her feelings for Fina are still stronger and that she is still in love with her. Marta refocuses on her work with the launch of the “Flor Divina” perfume in honor of Gervasio Merino; Pelayo returns to Toledo and is murdered. Darío tells Marta the whole truth. She begins the search that will lead to Fina's return and, finally, the most anticipated reunion of the series: MaFin together.

The third season of Sueños de Libertad began airing on Antena 3 on January 16, 2025, and is currently airing.
